Which Best Describes The Project?
The tool is intended to help someone understand and navigate which pathway is required, or best suited, for a solar project. The permit pathway required or chosen can depend on multiple factors, including maximum generation capacity (MW), disturbance area (acerage), and owner-operator of a public utility.
Nameplate Capacity: ≤ 5 MW
If the project is no larger than 5 MW, follow this pathway. The traditional permit application process is governed by the State Corporation Commission (SCC). As an option, you may choose to apply through the alternative process regulated by DEQ.
Nameplate Capacity: ≤ 5 MW
If the project is no larger than 5 MW, follow this pathway. The traditional permit application process is that governed by the State Corporation Comission (SCC). You may choose to instead apply through the alternative process of the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ).

Does It Meet The Exemption Criteria?
From 9VAC15-60-130A:

"The owner or operator of a small solar energy project is not required to submit any notification or certification to the VA Department of Environmental Quality if it meets at least one of the following criteria:

1. The small solar project falls within at least one of the following categories, without regard to the rated capacity or the disturbance zone of the project:
  • a. The small solar energy project is mounted on a single-family or duplex private residence.
  • b. The small solar energy project is mounted on one or more buildings less than 50 years old or, if 50 years of age or older, have been evaluated and determined by the Virginia Department of Historic Resources within the preceding seven years to be not VLR-eligible.
  • c. The small solar energy project is mounted over one or more existing parking lots, existing roads, or previously disturbed areas and any impacts to undisturbed areas do not exceed an additional two acres.
  • d. The small solar energy project utilizes integrated PV only, provided that the building or structure on which the integrated PV materials are used is less than 50 years old or, if 50 years of age or older, has been evaluated and determined by DHR within the preceding seven years to be not VLR-eligible."
Nameplate Capacity: > 5 MW but ≤ 100 MW
If the project is greater than 5 MW but less than or equal to 100 MW, follow this pathway. The traditional permit application process is governed by the State Corporation Comission (SCC). Follow the pathway that corresponds with the project's characteristics to determine whether the project is eligible for permit by rule through Virginia Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ).
Is This A Utility Regulated Pursuant To Title 56?
From § 10.1-1197.6-H of the Code of Virginia:

"Any small energy project shall be eligible for permit by rule under this section if the project is proposed, developed, constructed, or purchased by a person that is not a utility regulated pursuant to Title 56."
Alternative Process: DEQ
If the project is not a utility regulated to Title 56 or a utility regulated to Title 56 but meets the criteria laid out in § 10.1-1197.6 I.1 or I.2 of the Code of Virginia, the project is exempt from the SCC application requirement and is instead permitted to apply for the DEQ's Permit by Rule for Small Solar Energy Project.
Does It Meet The Criteria Laid Out In § 10.1-1197.6 I.1 Or I.2?
From § 10.1-1197.6-I of the Code of Virginia:

"Any small renewable energy project commencing operations after July 1, 2017, shall be eligible for permits by rule under this section and is exempt from State Corporation Commission environmental review or permitting in accordance with subsection B of § 10.1-1197.8 or other applicable law if the project is proposed, developed, constructed, or purchased by:

1. A public utility if the project's costs are not recovered from Virginia jurisdictional customers under base rates, a fuel factor charge under § 56-249.6, or a rate adjustment clause under subdivision A 6 of § 56-585.1.

2. A utility aggregation cooperative formed under Article 2 (§ 56-231.38 et seq.) of Chapter 9.1 of Title 56."
Alternative Process: DEQ
If the project met the criteria to arrive at the alternative process, the project is exempt from the SCC application requirement, and may instead apply for the DEQ's Permit by Rule for Small Solar Energy Project.
Nameplate Capacity: > 100 MW
If the project is greater than 100 MW, follow this pathway. The traditional permit application process is governed by the State Corporation Comission (SCC). Follow the pathway that corresponds with the project's characteristics to determine whether the project is eligible for permit by rule through Virginia Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ).
Is The Project ≤ 150 MW?
For permit by rule projects, Section § 10.1-1197.6-5 of the Code of Virginia states "the maximum generation capacity of the small renewable energy project by (i) an electrical generation facility that generates electricity only from sunlight or wind as designed does not exceed 150 megawatts."

Navigating the Virginia Solar Permit Map

The Virginia Permit Map is a central source of information for the solar permitting process in Virginia. The website is organized with a landing page to help the user determine which state permits are available for their project and detailed pages for each respective solar permit process and the interconnection process. For details on any step of any process, click on the respective box for further information. Upon determining which state permit options are available, the user should click on the permit name to find the associated permit page. Permit-specific pages provide details of the entire permit process: Federal, state, local requirements, and the interconnection map.
