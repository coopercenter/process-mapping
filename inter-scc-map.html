SGIP Level 1 General Process
Per 20VAC5-314-10 A, "A request to interconnect a certified small generating facility (SGF) no larger than 500 kilowatts (kW) shall be evaluated under the Level 1 process."
Submit SGIP Level 1 General Requirements
The SGIP Level 1 general requirements, as laid out in 20VAC5-314-40 B of the Virginia Administrative Code, are: "The IC shall submit (i) a complete Level 1 Interconnection Request Form (Schedule 5 of 20VAC5-314-170); (ii) site control documentation pursuant to Schedule 5 of 20VAC5-314-170; and (iii) the required $100 processing fee to the utility. Alternatively, the utility may require use of a commission-approved Interconnection Request Form similar to Schedule 5 of 20VAC5-314-170, which shall be made available to customers on the utility's website."

The utility will date-stamp and time-stamp the Interconnection Request Form upon receipt. This time is used to indicate queue position.
Utility notifies whether there are deficiencies in the submittal
Per 20VAC5-314-40 B, of the Virginia Administrative Code: "Within three business days of receiving the interconnection request, the utility shall notify the IC of receipt, which notification shall be made by United States mail, email address, or fax number provided by the IC. As soon as practicable after receipt, but not later than 10 business days after the date of receipt of the interconnection request, the utility shall notify the IC if there are any deficiencies in the IC's submittal. If there are deficiencies , such notice shall include a written list detailing all information that must be provided by the IC to complete the interconnection request."
If needed: Submit requested incomplete information
Per 20VAC5-314-40 B, of the Virginia Administrative Code: "The IC shall have 10 business days after receipt of the notice of incomplete information to submit the listed information or to request an extension of time to provide such information. The acceptance of such request for time extension shall be at the utility's discretion. If the IC does not provide the listed information or a request for an extension of time within the deadline, the Interconnection Request Form will be deemed withdrawn."
Enter queue window
Regulations surrounding queue numbers are established in 20VAC5-314-38, of the Virginia Administrative Code. Interconnection requests are assigned a queue number "based upon the date-stamp and time-stamp of receipt of a completed Interconnection Request Form by the utility." An Interconnection Request Form that is received earlier than another request shall be assigned a lower numerical queue number. Projects are studied in numerical order from low to high, except in the case of interdependent projects.
Evaluated by the utility for interdependency
Per 20VAC5-314-40 C, of the Virginia Administrative Code: “Within 15 business days after the date the IC submits a complete Interconnection Request Form and requisite fee, the utility shall evaluate the request to determine whether the IC's project is interdependent with one or more earlier-queued projects. If it is determined that the IC's project does not create an interdependency, the utility shall designate the IC's project as Project A, and, if so, shall inform the IC what utility modifications are required to interconnect the SGF. If the utility determines that the IC's project is interdependent with one or more earlier-queued projects, the utility may delay study of the IC request until the interdependency is resolved and the IC becomes a Project A as further addressed in 20VAC5-314-38 B. If the utility delays study of the IC's project, the utility shall notify the IC in writing and identify the number of earlier-queued interdependent projects.”
SGIP Level 2 General Process
Per 20VAC5-314-10 A, "A request to interconnect a certified small generating facility (SGF) no larger than 500 kilowatts (kW) shall be evaluated under the Level 1 process."
Submit SGIP Level 2 General Requirements
The SGIP Level 2 general requirements, as laid out in 20VAC5-314-50 A of the Virginia Administrative Code, are: "The IC shall submit (i) a completed Levels 2 and 3 Interconnection Request Form (Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170); (ii) site control documentation pursuant to Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170, and (iii) the required $1,000 processing fee."

The utility will date- and time-stamp the Interconnection Request Form upon receipt. This time is used to indicate queue position.

For interconnection requests initially evaluated at the Level 1 but transferred to the Level 2 process, see section I of 20VAC5-314-60
Utility notifies whether there are deficiencies in the submittal
Per 20VAC5-314-50 A of the Virginia Administrative Code: “Within 3 business days of receiving the interconnection request, the utility shall notify the IC of receipt, which notification shall be made by United States mail, email address, or fax number provided by the IC. The utility shall notify the IC within 10 business days of the receipt of the Interconnection Request Form and site control documentation as to whether the documentation is complete or incomplete. If the Interconnection Request Form is incomplete, the utility shall so notify the IC, including a written list detailing all information that must be provided by the IC to complete the Interconnection Request Form.”
If needed: Submit requested incomplete information
As laid out in 20VAC5-314-50 A, of the Virginia Administrative Code:
"The utility shall notify the IC within 10 business days of the receipt of the Interconnection Request Form and site control documentation as to whether the documentation is complete or incomplete. If the Interconnection Request Form is incomplete, the utility shall so notify the IC, including a written list detailing all information that must be provided by the IC to complete the Interconnection Request Form.” The IC then has “10 business days after receipt of the notice of incomplete information to submit the listed information or to request an extension of time to provide such information.” Extension shall not exceed an additional 10 business days.
Enter queue window
Regulations surrounding queue numbers are established in 20VAC5-314-38 of the Virginia Administrative Code. Interconnection requests are assigned a queue number "based upon the date-stamp and time-stamp of receipt of a completed Interconnection Request Form by the utility." An Interconnection Request Form that is received earlier than another request shall be assigned a lower numerical queue number. Projects are studied in numerical order from low to high, except in the case of interdependent projects.
Utility performs initial screen review and provides results
Per 20VAC5-314-60 B, of the Virginia Administrative Code: “Within 15 business days after the utility notifies the IC it has received a complete Interconnection Request Form, the utility shall perform an initial review using the screens set forth in subsection C or D of this section, as applicable and shall notify the IC of the results, including copies of the analysis and data underlying the utility's determinations under the screens.” Part C details screens for interconnections to radial circuits and Part D is on screens for interconnections involving networks.
SGIP Level 3 General Process
Per 20VAC5-314-10 A, "A request to interconnect a small generating facility (SGF) not qualifying for the Level 1 process (the request is greater than 500 kw) or Level 2 process (the request is greater than 2 MW), shall be evaluated under the Level 3 process."

The utility will date-stamp and time-stamp the Interconnection Request Form upon receipt. This time is used to indicate queue position.
Utility notifies whether there are deficiencies in the submittal

If the Interconnection Request Form is incomplete, the utility shall so notify the IC, including a written list detailing all information that must be provided by the IC to complete the Interconnection Request Form.”

"The utility shall notify the IC within 10 business days of the receipt of the Interconnection Request Form and site control documentation as to whether the documentation is complete or incomplete. Enter queue window

Interconnection requests are assigned a queue number "based upon the date-stamp and time-stamp of receipt of a completed Interconnection Request Form by the utility." An Interconnection Request Form that is received earlier than another request shall be assigned a lower numerical queue number. Projects are studied in numerical order from low to high, except in the case of interdependent projects. Interdependent projects follow a first come, first studied method. See 20VAC5-314-38.B for further information.
Scoping meeting
Per 20VAC5-314-70 B: of the Virginia Administrative Code:
"1. The purpose of the scoping meeting is to discuss the interconnection request and the utility's preliminary interdependency determination. The parties shall discuss the studies potentially required to safely and reliably interconnect the IC to the utility's system, including the cost responsibilities for the studies.
2. A scoping meeting shall be held no later than 10 business days after the Interconnection Request Form is deemed complete or as otherwise mutually agreed to in writing by the parties. The utility and the IC shall bring to the meeting all resources as may be reasonably required to accomplish the purpose of the meeting, such as system engineers and other personnel.
3. The scoping meeting may be omitted by mutual, written agreement of the parties."

Feasibility Study Process
The Feasibility Study is a relatively brief investigation into the proposed project to identify possible adverse system impacts. Results are used to determine which, if any, more in-depth studies are required (20VAC5-314-70).
Utility sends a Feasibility Study Agreement
Per 20VAC5-314-70 C.1 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "If the parties agree that a feasibility study should be performed, the utility shall provide the IC with a Feasibility Study Agreement (Schedule 7 of 20VAC5-314-170), including an outline of the scope of the feasibility study and an estimate of the cost to perform the study, no later than 10 business days after the scoping meeting or 10 business days after the decision is made to not have a scoping meeting and otherwise pursuant to subsection D of this section."
Execute the Feasibility Study Agreement
Per 20VAC5-314-70 C.2 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "To maintain its position in the utility's interconnection queue, the IC must execute the Feasibility Study Agreement, return it to the utility, and pay the interconnection request study deposit set forth in Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170 within 15 business days after receipt of the agreement. If the IC fails to return the executed Feasibility Study Agreement or make the full payment of the interconnection request study deposit within 15 business days after receipt of the agreement, the interconnection request shall be deemed withdrawn and the interconnection request shall lose its place in the utility's interconnection queue."
Perform feasibility study
A feasibility study is to be performed to "identify any potential adverse system impacts that would result from the interconnection of the SGF."

See Parts C.3-8 of 20VAC5-314-70 of the Virginia Administrative Code for feasability study requirements.
Utility delivers a feasibility study report
Per 20VAC5-314-70 C.10 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "A feasibility study report shall be prepared and transmitted to the IC within 30 business days of the utility's receipt of the complete executed Feasibility Study Agreement and required deposit."
System Impact Study Process
The System Impact Study is “an in-depth study to determine all electrical and physical interconnection requirements, the electrical-grid related impacts and mitigation of resultant overloads on existing electrical facilities, from the proposed project” (Virginia Department of Energy).
Utility sends a Feasibility Study Agreement
Per 20VAC5-314-70 C.1 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "If the parties agree to not perform a feasibility study, the utility shall provide the IC a System Impact Study Agreement (Schedule 8 of 20VAC5-314-170) including an outline of the scope of the study and an estimate of the cost to perform the study no later than 10 business days after the scoping meeting or five business days after the decision is made to not have a scoping meeting."
Execute the System Impact Study Agreement
Per 20VAC5-314-70 D.2 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "To maintain its position in the utility's interconnection queue, the IC must execute the System Impact Study Agreement, return it to the utility, and if applicable, pay the interconnection request study deposit set forth in Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170 within 15 business days after receipt of the agreement. If the IC fails to return the executed System Impact Study Agreement or make the full payment of the applicable interconnection request study deposit within 15 business days after receipt of the agreement, the interconnection request shall be deemed withdrawn, and the interconnection request shall lose its place in the utility's interconnection queue."
Perform system impact study
Per D.3-9 of 20VAC5-314-70 of the Virginia Administrative Code outline feasibility study requirements. See the code for further details.

The system impact study shall “identify and detail the electric system impacts that would result if the SGF were interconnected without project modifications or electric system modifications” as well as evaluating “the impact of the proposed interconnection on the reliability of the electric system.” This will be done using the results of the feasibility study, if applicable, and will consist of “a study of the potentially impacted transmission and distribution systems, a short circuit analysis, a stability analysis, a power flow analysis, voltage drop and flicker studies, grounding reviews, distribution load flow study, analysis of equipment interrupting ratings, protection coordination study, communications study, and impacts on electric system operation, as necessary.” Note: flicker studies due to voltage variation from cloud cover are required.

The system impact study must "provide a list of facilities and modifications that would be required as a result of the interconnection along with estimates of cost responsibility and time to construct.”
Utility transmits system impact study report
Per 20VAC5-314-70 D.9 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "A system impact study, if required, shall be completed and the results transmitted to the IC within 45 business days after the System Impact Study Agreement is signed by the parties."
Facilities Study Process
Interconnection, SCC Process, Facilities Study Process overview box (contains other boxes within it) Content: The Facilities Study develops cost estimates for all of the equipment, engineering, procurement, and construction needed to implement the required upgrades resulting from the feasibility impact study or system impact study. Note: This includes requirements for communications, which can bear significant costs (20VAC5-314-70).
Utility transmits system impact study report
Per 20VAC5-314-70 D.10 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "If the system impact study shows that facility modifications are needed to accommodate the SGF, then within 10 business days following transmittal of the system impact study report, the utility shall send the IC a Facilities Study Agreement (Schedule 9 of 20VAC5-314-170), including an outline of the scope of the study and an estimate of the cost to perform the study."
Utility transmits system impact study report
Per 20VAC5-314-70 E.2 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "To maintain its position in the utility's interconnection queue, the IC must execute the Facilities Study Agreement and return it to the utility and, if applicable, pay the interconnection request study deposit set forth in Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170 within 30 business days after receipt of the agreement, unless an extension has been agreed to in writing with the utility. Otherwise, the interconnection request shall be deemed withdrawn, and the interconnection request shall lose its place in the utility's interconnection queue."
Perform facilities study
Parts E.1-6 of 20VAC5-314-70 of the Virginia Administrative Code outline facilties study requirements. See the code for further details.

"The facilities study shall specify and estimate the cost of the equipment, engineering, procurement, and construction work needed to implement the conclusion of the feasibility impact study or system impact study and to allow the SGF to be interconnected and operate safely and reliably.” This involves the design for any required customer's interconnection facilities, attachment facilities, or upgrades.
Utility transmits system impact study report
Per 20VAC5-314-70 E.7 of the Virginia Administrative Code: "In cases where system upgrades are required, the utility shall transmit the facilities study report within 45 business days after receipt of the completed Facilities Study Agreement. In cases where no system upgrades are necessary and the required facilities are limited to the IC's interconnection facilities and attachment facilities only, the utility shall transmit the facilities study report within 30 business days after receipt of the completed Facilities Study Agreement."
Construction planning meeting
Per 20VAC5-314-70 F of the Virginia Administrative Code: "Within 15 business days of receipt of the report for the final study (i.e., the facilities study or, if applicable, a combined study that satisfies all study requirements), the IC shall request a construction planning meeting where failure to comply shall result in the interconnection request being deemed withdrawn. The construction planning meeting request shall be in writing and shall include the IC's reasonably requested date for completion of the construction of the customer's interconnection facilities and upgrades.

2. The construction planning meeting shall be scheduled within 15 business days of the request from the IC as stated in subdivision F 1 of this section, or as otherwise mutually agreed to in writing by the parties.

3. The purpose of the construction planning meeting is to identify the tasks for each party and discuss and determine the milestones for the construction of the system upgrades and attachment facilities. Agreed upon milestones shall be specific as to scope of action, responsible party, and dates of deliverables and shall be recorded in the SGIA (see Schedule 10 of 20VAC5-314-170) to be provided to the IC."
Decision Point
Per 20VAC5-314-70 C.11 of the Virginia Administrative Code: “If the feasibility study shows no potential for adverse system impacts, then within 10 business days of the completion of the study, the utility shall send the IC either an SGIA (Schedule 10 of 20VAC5-314-170) or a Facilities Study Agreement (Schedule 9 of 20VAC5-314-170), including an outline of the scope of the facilities study and an estimate of the cost to perform the study.”
