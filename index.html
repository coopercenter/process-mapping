Which Best Describes Your Project?
This tool is intended to help understand and navigate which pathway is required, or best suited, for a proposed solar project. The permit pathway required or chosen can depend on multiple factors, including maximum capacity (MW), disturbance area (acerage), and owner-operator of a public utility.
Nameplate Capacity: ≤ 5 MW
If the project is no larger than 5 MW, follow this pathway. The traditional permit application process is that governed by the State Corporation Comission (SCC). You may choose to instead apply through the alternative process of the Virginia Department of Environmental Quality (DEQ).
Alternative Process: Department of Environmental Quality
If choosing to apply through the DEQ, follow this pathway to determine which DEQ permit process that is required to be followed.

9VAC15-60-10 defines "disturbance zone" as: "The area within the site directly impacted by construction and operation of the solar energy project and within 100 feet of the boundary of the directly impacted area."
Project is ≤ 500 kW and the Disturbance Zone is ≤ 2 acres
If the project falls under this category, the project is exempt from the Small Solar PV Facility Notice of Intent (Section 130 Permit). Under 9VAC15-60-130A, "The owner or operator of a small solar energy project is not required to submit any notification or certification to the department."
Project is < 500 kW and the Disturbance Zone is > 2 acres but ≤ 10 acres
If your project falls under this category, you are exempt from the Small Solar PV Facility Notice of Intent (Section 130 Permit). Under VAC15-60-130A, "The owner or operator of a small solar energy project is not required to submit any notification or certification to the department."
