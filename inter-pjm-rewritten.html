PJM Interconnection Procedures
PJM Manual 14H

The following map represents the process by which a solar project may apply for interconnection through PJM. This process takes the form of a Cycle – the period of time between the start of an Application Phase and the conclusion of the corresponding Final Agreement Negotiation Phase. Each Cycle consists of the: Application Phase, Phase I, Decision Point I, Phase II, Decision Point II, Phase III, Decision Point III, and the Final Agreement Negotiation Phase. Within each of these phases, interconnection studies are performed to ensure the project meets the requirements necessary to interconnect to PJM. However, depending on PJM requirements and developers’ choices, interconnection requests may be subject to differing processes. The points at which the interconnection request may be withdrawn from the interconnection process or accelerated to the final agreement stage are displayed in this map. This map also represents the complexity of these studies, the costs associated, and relative timing. According to PJM, the entire process from the Application Deadline through the conclusion of the Final Agreement Negotiation Phase takes approximately 690 days. Note that applications must be created and models built prior to this Application Deadline, so in reality the process takes over 690 days.

Is the project interconnecting to FERC-jurisdictional facilities?
If the project is on FERC-jurisdictional facilities, follow the standard PJM process to receive a GIA. If the project is not on FERC-jurisdictional facilities, proceed to the WMPA process pursuant to PJM Manual 14H Section 8.5.5. Project Developers interconnecting to non-FERC jurisdictional facilities who intend to participate in the PJM wholesale market must follow the PJM process to receive a three party agreement known as a WMPA. The WMPA is essentially a GIA without interconnection provisions, and facilitates the wholesale sale of MWs from such facilities.
Obtain non-FERC jurisdictional interconnection agreement
In order to receive a WMPA, the developer must work independently with the relevant non-FERC jurisdictional entity to receive a non-FERC jurisdictional interconnection agreement. This means that the developer must go through the SCC process to get their interconnection approval, while also following PJM requirements to receive the WMPA. By Decision Point III, this interconnection agreement must be executed.
Follow WMPA Process
The developer must continue through the PJM process below to receive a WMPA. PJM studies are performed concurrently with state reviews. Note: project developers and utilities find it difficult to apply for approval through the SCC and PJM processes at the same time because the structure, timelines, and requirements differ greatly.
Application Phase
General Information
New service customers with generation greater than 20 MW are to follow the process outlined in PJM Manual 14H The steps in PJM Manual 14H are updated as of July 26, 2023. See PJM Manual 14H Section 1.2 to determine which customer classification fits your proposed project. The two customer classifications for which the process will be outlined are the most common customer designations: Generation Project Developer and the Eligible Customer.

Per PJM Manual 14H the Application Phase is the “Cycle period that includes both the submission and review of New Service Requests. The Project Developer or Eligible Customer submits an Application and PJM reviews and validates the submission and assigns a Project Identifier” (Page 28). Requirements of the Application phase can be found in Section 2 of PJM Manual 14H. Complete procedures are provided in Tariff, Part VII, Subpart C and Tariff, Part VIII, Subparts A and B.

Timeline: The Application Phase lasts approximately 90 days from the date of the Application Deadline. The time needed for the collection of required information and completion of the application occurs prior to Day 0 of this timeline.

NOTE: If instead the New Service Request meets one of the two criteria below per PJM Manual 14A follow the process outlined in PJM Manual 14A and PJM Tariff Part IV, Subpart A, Section 36
1. “Any New Service Request received prior to April 1, 2018.”
2. “Any New Service Request for which, as of the Transition Date (defined in Tariff, Part VII), the Interconnection Customer has received for execution an Interconnection Service Agreement or wholesale market participation agreement or has directed the PJM to file an Interconnection Service Agreement or wholesale market participation agreement unexecuted.”
Costs
Readiness Deposit 1
Per PJM Manual 14H Section 6.2 the Readiness Deposit No. 1 is due upon application submission. The amount will be $4,000 multiplied by the project size (in MW). This deposit must be provided through a wire transfer or a letter of credit.
Study Deposit
Per PJM Manual 14H Section 6.1 “a Study Deposit is a one-time deposit due at the beginning of the study process. The Study Deposit is non-binding, and actual study costs may exceed the Study deposit.

A Project Developer or Eligible Customer (collectively, applicant) submitting a New Service Request must provide a Study Deposit prior to the Application Deadline. The Study Deposit will range from $75,000 to $400,000 depending on the MW size of the project, as shown in the table below. The applicant will be responsible for paying actual study costs.” This deposit must be made by wire transfer.

Up to 90% of this deposit is refundable. If any refundable deposit monies remain after all costs and outstanding monies owed, per PJM Manual 14H Section 6.1.2.2, are covered, then the remaining amount will be refunded to the applicant.

“Actual study costs may exceed the Study Deposit amount collected. In these cases, PJM will notify the applicant of the shortfall amount. The applicant will have 20 Business Days to provide the additional funds or withdraw. If applicant fails to satisfy either of these requirements, PJM shall terminate and withdraw the New Service Request.”
Does the New Service Request move forward to Phase I?
Per PJM Manual 14H Section 2, the withdrawal of a New Service Request before the start of Phase I may occur at the request of a Project Developer or Eligible Customer or by PJM due to a failure to meet the Application Phase requirements.
