SGIP Level 1 General Process
Per 20VAC5-314-10 A, "A request to interconnect a certified small generating facility (SGF) no larger than 500 kilowatts (kW) shall be evaluated under the Level 1 process."
Submit SGIP Level 1 General Requirements
The SGIP Level 1 general requirements, as laid out in 20VAC5-314-40 B of the Virginia Administrative Code, are: "The IC shall submit (i) a complete Level 1 Interconnection Request Form (Schedule 5 of 20VAC5-314-170); (ii) site control documentation pursuant to Schedule 5 of 20VAC5-314-170; and (iii) the required $100 processing fee to the utility. Alternatively, the utility may require use of a commission-approved Interconnection Request Form similar to Schedule 5 of 20VAC5-314-170, which shall be made available to customers on the utility's website."

The utility will date-stamp and time-stamp the Interconnection Request Form upon receipt. This time is used to indicate queue position.
Utility notifies whether there are deficiencies in the submittal
Per 20VAC5-314-40 B, of the Virginia Administrative Code: "Within three business days of receiving the interconnection request, the utility shall notify the IC of receipt, which notification shall be made by United States mail, email address, or fax number provided by the IC. As soon as practicable after receipt, but not later than 10 business days after the date of receipt of the interconnection request, the utility shall notify the IC if there are any deficiencies in the IC's submittal. If there are deficiencies , such notice shall include a written list detailing all information that must be provided by the IC to complete the interconnection request."
If needed: Submit requested incomplete information
Per 20VAC5-314-40 B, of the Virginia Administrative Code: "The IC shall have 10 business days after receipt of the notice of incomplete information to submit the listed information or to request an extension of time to provide such information. The acceptance of such request for time extension shall be at the utility's discretion. If the IC does not provide the listed information or a request for an extension of time within the deadline, the Interconnection Request Form will be deemed withdrawn."
Enter queue window
Regulations surrounding queue numbers are established in 20VAC5-314-38, of the Virginia Administrative Code. Interconnection requests are assigned a queue number "based upon the date-stamp and time-stamp of receipt of a completed Interconnection Request Form by the utility." An Interconnection Request Form that is received earlier than another request shall be assigned a lower numerical queue number. Projects are studied in numerical order from low to high, except in the case of interdependent projects.
Evaluated by the utility for interdependency
Per 20VAC5-314-40 C, of the Virginia Administrative Code: “Within 15 business days after the date the IC submits a complete Interconnection Request Form and requisite fee, the utility shall evaluate the request to determine whether the IC's project is interdependent with one or more earlier-queued projects. If it is determined that the IC's project does not create an interdependency, the utility shall designate the IC's project as Project A, and, if so, shall inform the IC what utility modifications are required to interconnect the SGF. If the utility determines that the IC's project is interdependent with one or more earlier-queued projects, the utility may delay study of the IC request until the interdependency is resolved and the IC becomes a Project A as further addressed in 20VAC5-314-38 B. If the utility delays study of the IC's project, the utility shall notify the IC in writing and identify the number of earlier-queued interdependent projects.”
SGIP Level 2 General Process
Per 20VAC5-314-10 A, "A request to interconnect a certified small generating facility (SGF) no larger than 500 kilowatts (kW) shall be evaluated under the Level 1 process."
Submit SGIP Level 2 General Requirements
The SGIP Level 2 general requirements, as laid out in 20VAC5-314-50 A of the Virginia Administrative Code, are: "The IC shall submit (i) a completed Levels 2 and 3 Interconnection Request Form (Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170); (ii) site control documentation pursuant to Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170, and (iii) the required $1,000 processing fee."

The utility will date- and time-stamp the Interconnection Request Form upon receipt. This time is used to indicate queue position.

For interconnection requests initially evaluated at the Level 1 but transferred to the Level 2 process, see section I of 20VAC5-314-60
Utility performs initial screen review and provides results
Per 20VAC5-314-60 B, of the Virginia Administrative Code: “Within 15 business days after the utility notifies the IC it has received a complete Interconnection Request Form, the utility shall perform an initial review using the screens set forth in subsection C or D of this section, as applicable and shall notify the IC of the results, including copies of the analysis and data underlying the utility's determinations under the screens.” Part C details screens for interconnections to radial circuits and Part D is on screens for interconnections involving networks.
SGIP Level 3 General Process
Per 20VAC5-314-10 A, "A request to interconnect a small generating facility (SGF) not qualifying for the Level 1 process (the request is greater than 500 kw) or Level 2 process (the request is greater than 2 MW), shall be evaluated under the Level 3 process."
Submit SGIP Level 3 General Requirements
The SGIP Level 3 general requirements, as laid out in20VAC5-314-50 A, Code, are: "The IC shall submit (i) a completed Levels 2 and 3 Interconnection Request Form (Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170); (ii) site control documentation pursuant to Schedule 6 of 20VAC5-314-170, and (iii) the required $1,000 processing fee."

The utility will date-stamp and time-stamp the Interconnection Request Form upon receipt. This time is used to indicate queue position.
Scoping meeting
Per 20VAC5-314-70 B: of the Virginia Administrative Code:
"1. The purpose of the scoping meeting is to discuss the interconnection request and the utility's preliminary interdependency determination. The parties shall discuss the studies potentially required to safely and reliably interconnect the IC to the utility's system, including the cost responsibilities for the studies.
2. A scoping meeting shall be held no later than 10 business days after the Interconnection Request Form is deemed complete or as otherwise mutually agreed to in writing by the parties. The utility and the IC shall bring to the meeting all resources as may be reasonably required to accomplish the purpose of the meeting, such as system engineers and other personnel.
3. The scoping meeting may be omitted by mutual, written agreement of the parties."

Have the modifications been performed in compliance with 20VAC5-314-40 C.1?
According to 20VAC5-314-40 C of the Virginia Administrative Code: “If the interconnection can be accomplished with minor modifications (e.g., changing meters, fuses, and relay settings) to the utility system, the IC and the utility may informally agree upon a plan to effectuate the required installations and modifications. The utility shall perform all installations and modifications of the utility system and the IC shall reimburse the utility for the cost of such installations and modifications. The IC shall perform all required modifications to its SGF.”

If instead either of the following situations occur, the interconnection request will be transferred to the Level 2 process or handled according to 20VAC5-314-100 at the IC’s option:
  1. 20VAC5-314-40 C.2: “If the interconnection cannot be accomplished with minor modifications or the parties cannot come to an agreement regarding modifications to the utility system within 10 business days of study results being provided by the utility.”
  2. 20VAC5-314-40 C.3: “If the utility cannot reasonably determine that the modifications to the utility's system can be completed without additional study, the utility shall provide the IC, in writing, justification for that determination.”
Satisfy the additional requirements of 20VAC5-314-40 D
As laid out in 20VAC5-314-40 D of the Virignia Administrative Code:
An IC seeking to interconnect under the Level 1 interconnection process “may begin operation of an SGF when any required modifications or additions provided for in subsection C of this section are complete and when the [seven] additional requirements are satisfied." See 20VAC5-314-40 D for the details of these additional requirements.
Passed: Utility provides SGIA
Per 20VAC5-314-60 E of the Virginia Administrative Code: “If the interconnection passes the screens, the interconnection request shall be approved and the utility shall provide the IC an SGIA within 10 business days after the determination.”

NOTE: The subsequent SGIA is the end of the process for this branch of the decision node.
Failed: Utility provides SGIA
Per 20VAC5-314-60 F of the Virginia Administrative Code: “If the interconnection fails any screens, but the utility determines that the SGF may nevertheless be interconnected consistent with safety, reliability, and power quality standards, the utility shall provide the IC an SGIA within 10 business days after the determination.”

NOTE: The subsequent SGIA is the end of the process for this branch of the decision node.

As laid out in 20VAC5-314-60 G of the Virginia Administrative Code:
“If the interconnection fails any screen and the utility determines that the interconnection cannot be approved without (i) minor modifications at minimal cost; (ii) a supplemental review or other additional studies or actions; or (iii) incurring significant cost to address safety, reliability, or power quality problems, the utility shall notify the IC and provide copies of the data and analyses underlying its conclusion within 10 business days after that determination. Within 10 business days of the determination, the utility shall offer to convene a customer options meeting to review possible IC facility modifications, or the screen analysis and related results, to determine what further steps are needed to permit the SGF to be connected safely and reliably. At the time of notification of the utility's determination, or at the customer options meeting, the utility shall:
1. Offer to perform facility modifications or minor modifications to the utility system (e.g., changing meters, fuses, and relay settings) and provide an estimate of the cost to make such modifications to the utility system. If the IC agrees to pay for the modifications to the utility's electric system, the utility shall provide the IC with an SGIA within 10 business days after the customer options meeting;
2. Offer to perform a supplemental review in accordance with subsection H of this section and provide the IC with information on the studies potentially required to be performed under the supplemental review, and an estimate of the costs and timing of such review; or
3. Obtain the IC's agreement to continue evaluating the interconnection request, but under the Level 3 interconnection process.”
Work with the utility to resolve issues
In order to complete the Level 2 Process and receive an SGIA, the IC must resolve issues by working with the utility following the process outlined in 20VAC15-314-60 G.1 or 20VAC15-314-60 G.2 .
